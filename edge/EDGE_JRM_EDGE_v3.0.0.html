Consistency Gate checks whether your claims agree or contradict each other.
Add statements ("claims"), draw relationships between them, and score the graph. Contradictions raise the score; agreements lower it. High scores trigger alerts and suggest fixes.
Load a sample scenario with contradicting claims, score it, and see drift signals + patch suggestions instantly. How does this work? ▼
How the Consistency Gate Works
1. Add Claims
Enter statements from different sources. Each claim has a key (ID), text (what it says), authority (who said it), and confidence (how sure).
2. Connect Them
Draw edges between claims: contradicts (they disagree), supports (they agree), or depends (one relies on the other).
3. Score
Hit Score to compute the CRS (0-100). Higher = more disagreement. Contradictions raise it, supports lower it. Stale claims fade over time.
4. Act
If the score is high, the system emits drift signals (alerts) and suggests patches (e.g. expire a stale claim, request more evidence). You can simulate patches before applying.
What does "residue" mean?
Residue is the unresolved disagreement between two connected claims — think of it as tension on a wire. When "CPU is low" contradicts "CPU is high", that contradiction creates residue (leftover conflict the system can't reconcile). Agreements reduce residue. The CRS (Consistency Residue Score) is the total residue across all connections, normalized to 0-100. High residue = lots of unresolved contradictions = something needs attention.
Two scoring modes: Basic scores purely by edge type and weight. Sheaf also considers how similar the claim texts are — if two "contradicting" claims actually say nearly the same thing, the conflict score is reduced.
