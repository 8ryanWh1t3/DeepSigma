AuthorityOps — The Trust Boundary

AuthorityOps enforces who can do what, in what state, under what authority. It is the hard law layer — non-negotiable, non-configurable, non-bypassable.

The 5 Hard Laws

Law 1No self-certification of closure
Law 2No closure without owner + patch + evidence
Law 3No overwrite of historical canon — append, version, reopen only
Law 4Authority must be role-bound and state-bound
Law 5No agent can self-promote its authority level

Authority Levels

0
Manual — Human decides, machine executes. Full human control.
1
Advisory — Machine proposes, human approves. Human-in-the-loop.
2
Supervised — Machine decides within policy bounds, human monitors. Human-on-the-loop.
3
Autonomous + Audit — Machine decides and executes, human reviews post-hoc. Every episode sealed.
4
Governed Autonomy — Machine decides, executes, and self-corrects within sealed CERPA episodes. Full provenance. AuthorityOps enforces boundaries.

The Key Insight

The governance envelope is model-agnostic. It wraps the decision, not the model. When you swap from GPT-4 to Claude to a local GGUF model, the decisions, their provenance, their evidence, and their institutional memory persist. The model is a thruster. CERPA is the spacecraft.
