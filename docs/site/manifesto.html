The Reference Layer Manifesto

Three Failures

Decision Amnesia

Agents act, but nothing remembers why. A model quarantines an account, escalates a ticket, or approves a trade. Seconds later the reasoning is gone.

Authority Vacuum

No one can prove who authorized the action. The chain from human intent to machine execution is undocumented, unverifiable, and invisible.

Silent Drift

The system changes behavior and nobody notices. Without continuous measurement the first sign of drift is a production incident.

What a Reference Layer Does

A reference layer sits between the agent runtime and the outside world. Every decision passes through four artifacts:

ArtifactPurpose
Episode SealingTamper-evident decision log with cryptographic hashes, timestamps, actors, and outcomes
Authority BindingNamed grants with scope and expiry, provable for any claim at any time
Drift DetectionTyped signals with severity, fingerprint, and recommended patch type
Claim LifecycleValidation, authority check, graph recording, and drift emission in one pipeline

The Contract

Intent is explicit

Every decision records what the agent planned to do and why.

Authority is verified

Every claim traces back to a named grant with scope, source, and expiry.

Logic is auditable

The full provenance chain is retrievable for any decision at any time.

Outcomes are sealed

Cryptographic hashes bind the record to its content. Tampering breaks the chain.
